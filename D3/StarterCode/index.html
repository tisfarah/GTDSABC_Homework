Correlations Discovered Between Age and Smoking Rates

There is a positive correlation between Age and Smoking Rate in this dataset. Utah has the lowest Smoking rate at 9.7 and West Virginia has the highest Smoking rate at 26.7. At surface level, there may be cultural implications to these rates. The state of Utah has a very large Mormon population. Mormons have low rates of drinking alcohol and smoking tobacco. On the other hand, West Virginia has historically had large amounts of tobacco fields and was a major exporter of tobacco products.
